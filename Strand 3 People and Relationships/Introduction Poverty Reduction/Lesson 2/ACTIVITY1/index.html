Activity Introduction

Look closely. Our environment is trying to tell us something. In these images, you will see examples of how natural resources are being used beyond their limits. Tap each picture to reveal explanations about the damage caused and its impact on people who depend on these resources. Let us learn how overuse can affect both nature and communities.

Learner Instructions

Exploration Summary

Challenge Explanation
Deforestation This area has been cleared of trees without replanting, causing deforestation. Without trees, the soil loses its strength and can wash away in heavy rains. Animals lose their homes, and rainfall patterns can change, making farming more difficult.
Overfishing Taking too many fish or using cages that block natural water flow disrupts the balance of the ecosystem. Fish numbers drop, harming the food supply and income for fishing communities.
Over-Irrigation Using too much water for farming drains rivers and underground water sources. Over time, soil can become too salty or cracked, making it harder for crops to grow.
Strip Mining Digging away large areas of land for minerals leaves behind bare, unusable ground. Farming becomes impossible, wildlife disappears, and communities may have to move away.
