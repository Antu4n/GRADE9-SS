Activity Introduction

Let us connect the dots! Poverty does not happen all by itself, it grows from problems that cause even more problems. In this activity, your challenge is to match each cause with its real-life effect. Just like linking puzzle pieces, you will see how one event like a flood or poor leadership can lead to hunger, job loss, or even conflict.

Learner Instructions

  1. Look at the cause cards at the bottom of the screen.
  2. Drag each cause card to its matching real-life effect in the grid above.
  3. After placing each card, check the feedback to see if you made the right connection.
  4. Use the hints in the feedback to correct your matches if needed.

Key Takeaways

Cause / Root Real-Life Effect
Lack of Education Limited access to quality education makes it hard to get well-paying jobs.
Unemployment Lack of proper skills and jobs naturally leads to having no income.
Natural Calamities Droughts, floods, and extreme weather destroy crops and community livelihoods.
Poor Governance Bad leadership and corruption waste vital resources and hinder development.
Political Instability Unfairness and mistreatment often lead to conflict, disrupting lives and economies.
Summary Poverty is complex, but understanding its causes is the first step toward solving it.
