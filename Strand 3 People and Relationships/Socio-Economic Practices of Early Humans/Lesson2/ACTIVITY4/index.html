Key Takeaways

Concept Detail
Periods The Stone Age is divided into three periods: Early, Middle, and Late Stone Age, each with different types of tools.
Early Stone Age Tools (like choppers and hand axes) were large and basic, used for cutting and hunting.
Middle Stone Age Tools (like scrapers and spear points) became smaller, sharper, and more specialized.
Late Stone Age Tools (like microliths and polished axes) were highly refined and sometimes combined with other materials (wood/bone).
Function & Evolution Each tool had a specific function. Tool development shows how early humans became more skilled, intelligent, and organized.
