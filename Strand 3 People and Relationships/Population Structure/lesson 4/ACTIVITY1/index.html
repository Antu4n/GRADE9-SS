Activity Introduction

Let us explore how different age groups shape what a country needs most. On this diagram, you will see three population groups: children, working-age adults, and the elderly. Click each group to find out what key services they need, like schools, jobs, and hospitals. Then, see how governments use this information to plan and distribute national resources. Ready? Click to explore!

Learner Instructions

  1. Look at the population pyramid displayed on your screen. It is divided into three coloured sections:
    • Blue – Children (0–14 years)
    • Yellow – Working-age adults (15–64 years)
    • Red – Elderly (65+ years)
  2. Click on a coloured section's bracket to explore that age group’s needs. A pop-up will appear with key services and resources needed.
  3. Explore all three age groups to discover what services they need and how governments allocate resources.
