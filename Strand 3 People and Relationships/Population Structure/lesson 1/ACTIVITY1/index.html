Activity Introduction

Attention, Population Detective! You are about to take on your first big challenge matching each type of population data source to its correct description. You will see names like census, survey, and vital statistics. Your job is to drag each name to the description that best explains what it does. Ready to investigate? Let us match and reveal the truth behind the data!

Learner Instructions

  1. Drag each population data source to the description that matches it best.
  2. Think about what each source collects and how it is used, does it count everyone, track life events, come from everyday services, or compare countries?
  3. Drop your choice to see instant feedback. Keep matching until every source is paired with its correct description!

Key Takeaways

Concept Detail
Population Census Full count of a population, conducted at regular intervals.
Sample Survey Data from a portion of the population to estimate general trends.
Vital Statistics Continuous records of births, deaths, and marriages.
Administrative Records Data from institutions like schools, hospitals, and immigration offices.
Government Agencies Organizations like KNBS or Destatis that collect and manage official data.
International Publications Reports and statistics shared by global bodies like the UN, World Bank, etc.
